British: Crime and punishment

Posted on 2017-10-30   |   In assignment   |  

[name=Garnix, 102062314] [time=Sat, April 15, 2017 9:50] [color=#907bf7]

Q1: According to the documentary, what is the characteristics of the crime during each period?

  • There are four stages being separated in the documentary. The middle age before 1450, the so called Dark Ages, which is the time that human could only record data manually and roughly, and the crimes were seen to be more simple comparing to other times. Maybe it is why the judge procession would be quicker then modern court cases. The purer the cases could be settled, the clearer the punishment could be defined. Also, it was the time being more lawless, the manorialism still constructed the major social system. Lords and the kings have the power to affect the final decisions, which was a factor that leaded to the dark figures. Theft occupied approximately 3/4 crimes then, certificating that theft was emphasized and considered a serious crime of a servant who belonged to a lord.
  • The second is from 1450 to 1750, the so called Age of Discovery, which European countries including England became more prosperous, and thus got a new tensions toward society. The more complicated commercial activities also cause the crime diversification, such as smuggled. Naturally, the crime cases also reflect the social atmosphere, like religion loyalty (heresy, Witchcraft),and the government unstable system(Treason ). It could also be a note that people who don’t have a regular job or a fixed home would be a crime, but they would get pension contrarily nowadays.
  • The third period is form 1750 to 1900, which could directly involve the Industrial Revolution, which cause the whole Britain, even the whole world a tremendous changes. The population growing up, the changes of working environments and types, the fast transportation of human and the increasing number of immigrations to cities. The most worth is that newspaper and the photography popped up, and the most specific crime that everyone of in that time may be the employing children under age, which was difficult to prevent.
  • The final stage refers to the 20th century, the average income was promoted but the wealth inequality became worse. Significant events like the invention of internet, cars..etc, the two World Wars, the revolution of the governments, all brought the new styles of crimes(especially the aspects of technology and inventions). I would like to note that a report published in the newspaper(in 2nd case) said that half British ‘crime’ is by motorists, the unexpected dark figures which was again reminded in this period and the decline of importance of the traditional religion.

Q2: Usually, we believe the poverty and hunger is the cause to the crimes, but with the wide spread of education, the more building of playing fields, child guidence centres, why were so many young males involved with crime?

  • From the case 1, the first coming factor referred to the wars. Frequent violent events stimulate the war spirits that trigger the desire of commiting a crime, the big request of soilders that leaded to the lack of fathers, teachers, police and the social workers, the cinema that involved crime issued which may encourage people to immitate the ‘romantic and adventurous actions’.
  • Another point worth the attention is the change of the moral standard, which could throughly affect ones’ behaviors and the thoughts. In case 2, when children were educated that being powerful and dominating is more important than anything, they would try to win over to fulfill the expectations and achievements of his/her parents and more seriously, her-/himself. They appear to give a legitimate excuse for every actions that violate the laws, sometimes even thought the act of a brave and honoured thing. We used to think that poverty could dominate the crime rate of a society, but there also some reports(case 3) point out that unlimited money given by parents and the improper concepts passing down to the young could also be a dangerous hazard toward the whole society.
  • Last but not the least, and the most influential factor in my opinions, that is exactly the generation that are left by wars. The terrific aftermath causing in their childhood may lead to an unexpected shadow that we can’t imagine. The period, to be more specifically, the young who experience the two world wars are called the Lost Generation, 1893-1900. They juvenile doubt the world, hate the world, fight against everything but don’t know who they are. It’s also the most prosperous times which could be called Roaring Twenties in regards to the literature, music, economic, movies, every aspects in life. From North America to Europe, the fast growing of economic let the emerging rich could live a voluptuous life (mentioned above). In the well-known The Great Gatsby written by Francis Scott Key Fitzgerald, the plots that depict the crime culture are exactly under the background of the Lost Generation, reflecting the cruel and the real fact of the times.
