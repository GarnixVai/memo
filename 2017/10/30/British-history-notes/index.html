1066-1189 The Norman Kings and Henry II![]

  • The Norman Kings

    • William I 1066-1087 (21 years)
    • William II ‘Rufus’ 1087-1100 (13)
    • Henry I 1100-1135 (35)
    • Stephen(Henry’s neice) 1135-54 (19)
    • Henry II 1154-1189 (35): the first Angevin king

  • Scrambling for the throne(not always successful)

    • Treasury(country’s assess)
    • Coronation charter (religion)
    • Disputes between father and offspring
  • Institutional development
    • Itinerant court
      • movable(unstable ,developing)
      • 13th : kings start to settle down
      • Three institutions
        • The Chancery:(11th 12th)documentary, secretary… 文書活動
        • The Exchequer: 財務部
        • The Justiciar: 郡(之後慢慢取消)/全國 司法事務
          開始出現中央政府機構
  • Stephen dynasty: civil war
    • The ‘Anarchy’: non government
  • ‘Restoring the laws and customs of Henry I’s time’
    • Actually re-invented government
  • The sucession and system
    • stage1: Land as gift
    • stage2: Knight service: redefining the terms of landholding
      • scutage; ‘feudal incidents’
    • stage3: ‘Military tenure’ in land law
      • Land sale = enfeoffment

Kings

  • Willian I 1066-1087
    Start to invade Wales and Scotland
    • Coronation: from duke to the anointed king
      • Anglo-Saxon royal authority
    • Rebellions 1067-1072
      • ‘Harrying of the North’, Hereward the Wake; castles
    • Domesday Book 1086
      • 土地調查記錄(末日審判書–Christian): 科學式、系統式的統治
  • William II ‘Rufus’ 1087-1100
    祖產隨老大(諾曼第)、新取得土地給予老二、父子關係不好
    • William I’s eldest son Robert Curthose was in exile in 1087
      - Rebellion 1078-80, 1084-87
      • Patrimony (duchy of Normandy)/ acquisition (kingdom of England)
      • Divided loyalty of the aristocracy
    • 1096 Duke Robert mortgaged Normandy to William II to fund his crusade
      • 因十字軍而將土地租給弟弟(諾曼第與英格蘭統治權合併)

  • Henry I 1100-1135

    • Henry V, Duke Robert was still on crusade when William II died
      • 1106 battle of Tinchebrai: Henry defeated Robert
    • 1100 coronation charter: not to keep bishoprics vacant
      • Free election after receiving a ‘licence to elect’ from the king
      • Temporalities would be taken into the king’s custody, until the bishop-elect did homage to the king
      • The Investiture Controversy in England
    • The exchequer(發明財政部, chequer:棋)
      利用控制財政來掌握地方官
      • Twice yearly accounting procedure to keep the sheriffs under control
      • County farm, judicial proceeds etc
    • The justiciar and the eyre
      • justiciar: 可代理國王
      • eyre: overlaps
    • Family and succession crisis
      • Emperor of the Romans (d.1125); 2) Geoffrey, Count of Anjou (m.1128)
      • Son William was killed in the White Ship incident in 1120
      • Daughter Matilda. Was married to
        don't call me ma'dam , call me queen
        (1) Henry V, Emperor of the Romans(die in 1125) : then goes back to hometown
        (2) Geoffrey, Count of Anjou(m 1128): 安茹伯爵
    • 1127, 1131 general oaths accepting Matilda as Henry’s heir

  • Stephen 1135-1154(叔舅關係)

    • Younger son of Henry I’s sister Adela and the French count of Blois, Stephen
      • Matilda was away from court when Henry I died
    • Coronation charter: promised to limit the extension of forest law
      地主被限制諸多特權、森林法規
      • As news of Henry’s death spread, the king’s deer were slaughtered, forest fences were broken down across England
    • Civil war from 1137(1135+2)
      • Normandy joined the Angevin camp in 1141
      • 1140s stalemate. Property disputes, private alliances and private castles
      • 1153 Treaty of Westminster: Stephen recognized Matilda’s son Henry as heir

        The The Anglo-Saxon Chronicle, s.a. 1137

  • Henry II 1154-1189
    領地 南:比里牛斯山 北:蘇格蘭

    • Mom and nobles are in wars
    • Father: Geoffrey, count of Anjou (d. 1151)
      • Angevin/Plantagenet dynasty
    • Wife: Eleanor, duchess of Aquitaine 亞奎丹 (m. 1152)
      貴族女孩繼承>>>王權女孩繼承
    • 1166 acquired Brittany
    • 1171 Lordship of Ireland(取得愛爾蘭宗主權: 因為理查)
      • 1166 Dermot MacMurrough, king of Leinster, sought help from Anglo-Norman Marcher lords against other Irish kings. Dermot died in 1171; Richard Strongbow, earl of Pembroke, set himself up at Leinster.
      • Henry went to assert his authority over native Irish kings and Anglo-Norman lords
    • Multi-centred: Westminster, Rouen, Poitiers
    • Literary achievements of the court circle(著名文學家)
    • Restoring order after the civil war. legal reforms: the birth of the common law(因內戰而留下的衝突需要一套系統處理)
    • Thomas Becket, archbishop of Canterbury was murdered in 1170
      • Could the king’s courts judge clerical malefactors?
      • Canonized in 1173

0. In Different regions

  • In France

    • c.1000 stone tower:Château de Langeais, built by Fulk Nerra, Count of Anjou in c. 994
    • Squared stones filled with rubble and mortar;
      Building in stone continued in the post-Roman period
    • c.1000 motte and bailey
      • Ditch and earth rampart with palisade
      • Enclosure (bailey)
        • Accommodation for soldiers, chapel, storage place, the great hall
      • A great mound of earth (motte)
        bailey composed motte, with soil and stone
        • Wooden palisade and tower on top of the motte
          • Defensive and offensive (aircraft carrier): a base for army
      • To show off, to overawe

  • In Anglo-Saxon England

    • Burhs
      • Alfred and his children. Planned, fortified towns
      • Architechture referecne
    • 1050s Normans in England(French styles)
      • Herefordshire (Ewyas Harold Castle, Hereford Castle)
    • and Essex (Clavering Castle)

  • In Normandy before 1066

    • Most castles were built in the motte-and-bailey style between 1035-1047, by anyone who could command enough manpower and material
      • Duke Richard III (1016-27) – brother Robert I (1027-35) – son William II (aged 8 in 1035)

  • Motte-and-baily(wooden) castles in England
    Motte:露天保護 baily: garten庭院

    • King William I: upon landing, built Pevensey and Hastings, and then Dover and others
      • Alternate layers of soil and stone, timber structure on top
    • Royal licence
      • Nobel no longer has the right to build castles by themselves
      • E.g. Roger of Montgomery’s Hen Domen castle
    • Plain but serviceable buildings. No jewellery.
      • Norman soldiers dont favor jewellery on the battle field like English.
    • Over 600? Continued to be inhabited/built along the Welsh borders, in Ireland and the Scottish borders

  • Stone keeps(10th has been in France)

    • 1070s: town castles after the uprisings
      • More striking. Anglo-Saxon for building: timbrian
      • more in cities
    • 1140s: Civil War in Stephen’s reign
    • Stone as the main building material
      • Bailey – surrounded by stone curtain wall
      • Motte and timber tower –
        • stone shell keep: Wiston Castle, Pembrokeshire; Restormel Castle, Cornwall
        • stone tower keep (= donjon, dungeon): Tower keep and curtain wall: Conisbrough Castle, Yorkshire

  • Tower keep

    • 1G: rectangular Norwich Castle, the White Tower, Colchester Castle
    • 2G: tall and slender, with a forebuilding
      • protecting
      • Castle Rising
      • The Archbishop of Canterbury’s Rochester Castle
        • 125 feet, built in 1127-35
        • Interior. Hedingham Castle: four storeys, central cross wall
    • 3G: round or polygonal (corner) towers
  • Caernarfon Castle
    • 以室內空間為主
    • 威爾斯親王(英國王室長子)
    • Imperial
    • eagles symbolize power in that era
    • 13th century(深色條紋、平行漸層)–>當時君士坦丁堡的特色之一
  • Late Medieval Castles
    • contain few feteaturs of a castle

  • Functions

    • Castles in more peaceful times
      • administration (residences for sheriffs, prisons, treasuries for the king), hunting, travel and trade
    • Stone walls and stone keeps at war
      • Battering ram – forebuilding
      • Ladders, belfries – higher walls, moat
      • Fire – stone walls and lead roofs
      • Trebuchet – thicker walls and the batter(似斜坡)
      • Mining – worked when King John laid siege to Rochester
      • Cutting off supply line – waterfront location(攻城最常用的戰術:截斷補給線)
    • Archers and round corner towers, round mural towers

  • functions and form

    • Fortresses
      • battlemens = cremenation
      • machicolation(使弓箭手可向下射箭)
      • arrow-loops(使弓箭手可)
